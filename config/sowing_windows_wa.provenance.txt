--- METHOD -----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Table 15 colour-coded cells -> per-maturity window (week bands), week->DOY with
   wk1=1-7, wk2=8-14, wk3=15-21, wk4=22-EOM; 2026 non-leap (Apr1=91, May1=121, Jun1=152):
     maturity      earliest  optimum         latest_viable  (DOY: earliest/opt_start/opt_end/latest)
     slow          Apr wk2   Apr wk3-May wk1  May wk2        98 /105/127/134
     mid_slow      Apr wk3   Apr wk4-May wk2  May wk3       105 /112/134/141
     quick_mid     May wk1   May wk2-wk4      Jun wk1       121 /128/151/158
     quick         May wk2   May wk3-Jun wk1  Jun wk2       128 /135/158/165
   (very_slow and Winter-wheat rows exist but are excluded -- see assumptions.)
2. Each 2025 variety (Table 1) mapped to its Table 15 maturity row (Table 15 variety lists).
3. Window boundary DOYs area-weighted by 2025 maturity-class share, then rounded:
     earliest_sow_doy=120 (Apr 30) · optimal_start_doy=127 (May 7) ·
     optimal_end_doy=150 (May 30) · latest_viable_doy=157 (Jun 6)

--- REPRESENTED-AREA COVERAGE ---------------------------------------------
  Table 1 listed varieties total                : 98.7% of 2025 area (it omits <0.2%)
  Mapped to a Table 15 maturity (drives window) : 91.3%
  Maturity mix of the mapped area:
     quick_mid 63.7% (69.8% of mapped) · quick 15.8% (17.3%) ·
     mid_slow 10.0% (11.0%) · slow 1.8% (2.0%)
  Winter wheat (excluded)                       :  0.3% (Mowhawk)
  Unmapped (not in Table 15 variety lists)      :  7.1%
     LRPB Havoc, Chief CL Plus, Machete, Mace, LRPB Avenger, Westonia, Arrino, Magenta
     (older/minor varieties; no maturity row stated -> not assigned, not guessed)

--- ASSUMPTIONS & THRESHOLDS ----------------------------------------------
  - Single window per commodity: the swp-1 schema has no maturity column, so the
    maturity distribution is collapsed to one window (area-weighted).
  - Statewide weights: Table 1 shares are WA-wide; the same window is replicated
    across all 7 BEN Agri WA SDs (the guide encodes no sub-state numeric windows -- D2).
  - Winter wheat EXCLUDED (Table 15 footnote: applicable only to southern WA).
  - very_slow excluded from the weighting (0% area in 2025).
  - Unmapped 7.1% excluded from the weighting (not redistributed) -- conservative;
    they are mostly quick/quick-mid older types, so including them would shift the
    window little.
  - FROST NOT MODELLED: Table 15 is explicitly "assumes low frost risk". Frost-driven
    sowing delay is out of scope for v1 (and belongs in a future evidence layer, not here).

--- CONFIDENCE -------------------------------------------------------------
  confidence = medium (raised from low). Basis: faithful Table 15 extraction +
  Table 1 area weighting covering 91.3% of 2025 area. NOT high because it is a
  single-window maturity collapse, statewide (not per-SD), and frost-agnostic.

--- CAVEATS (carry to merge) ----------------------------------------------
  1. wheat is a maturity-collapsed, 2025-area-weighted single window.
  2. southern-only winter wheat excluded.
  3. statewide weights applied uniformly to all 7 SDs (no per-SD maturity mix).
  4. frost timing not considered (Table 15 assumes low frost risk).
  5. a future schema/version could carry maturity class or per-SD weights before
     tightening further.

COMMODITY CODE: guide "Wheat" -> BEN Agri `wheat_incl_durum`
  (spec 7.2 / config/guide_commodity_map.yaml).

--- OTHER CROPS (barley, canola, lupins, oats, + pulses) -------------------
  Investigated (step 6): a full-PDF scan for a sowing-time calendar grid (the
  wk1..wk4 colour-coded signature) finds it ONLY on the wheat page (p31). Barley,
  canola, lupins and oats have NO equivalent suggested-sowing-times table -- only
  scattered, observational/variety-specific prose (e.g. oats "target April sowing";
  canola NVT sown "24 Apr-11 May"; barley "planting windows from late May to early
  June"). Deriving per-commodity DOY windows from prose would be interpolation
  beyond what the guide states, so per the review brief these crops are DEFERRED
  (not drafted). They remain candidates if a future source provides an extractable
  table or an externally-supplied window set.
