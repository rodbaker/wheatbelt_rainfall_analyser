## Read

- **Statewide reversal.** On the full Apr→Jun-23 window, the SA wheat belt went from
  **decile 1.0 (record-dry, 58% of median) in 2025 to decile 9.5 (143%) in 2026** —
  58 mm → 141 mm, roughly **2.4×**.
- **Not just a late save.** Apr–May alone flipped decile **1.4 → 6.7** (24 → 67 mm,
  ~2.8×): 2026 was already above-average before June, then June pushed it to very wet.
- **Every major zone flips to decile 9–10** on the full window (Eyre, Yorke, Murray
  Lands, South East all decile 10; Northern decile 9). In 2025 the same zones were
  decile 1.0–1.9.
- **Outer Adelaide is the lone laggard** — decile 5.2 (95% of median), merely average
  rather than wet, but small (59 kha).

Consistent with the June 17 fact-check ("much more favourable start than last year"),
now firmer: decile 9.0 at mid-June → **9.5** through the 23rd. Last year near the driest
on record; this year near the wettest.
