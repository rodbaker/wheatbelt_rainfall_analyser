# June 2026 rainfall — state-by-state write-up with SA2 evidence

*Generated 2026-06-25. SILO gridded rainfall, 1–23 June 2026 (month-to-date through
day 23), aggregated to wheat-growing Statistical Divisions / SA2s and area-weighted by
wheat planting (2020–21 universe). Deciles rank against the 2005–2025 June climatology
scaled to the same 23-day window (×0.767). "% of median" is the wheat-weighted total
against that scaled baseline.*

*Three reading windows are used where season context matters:*
- ***Jun*** *— 1–23 June only (the month-to-date pulse).*
- ***Mar→*** *— Mar 1 → Jun 23 (full-season profile incl. summer/autumn).*
- ***May→*** *— May 1 → Jun 23 (the establishment window, recent rain).*

## National picture

A decisively wet June across the southern cropping belt, tapering north. The three
southern states — WA, SA, Victoria — are all running at or near record-wet June pace
(decile 9–10, roughly twice median). The gradient turns marginal moving into NSW's
north and Queensland, where June has been closer to — or below — average and the season
leans on a thinner moisture profile.

## Western Australia — decile 10, 218% of median (62 mm)

An exceptional June for the state, with one real internal split. The bulk of the crop is
very wet: **Central** (1,029 kha) at 277% of median, the **Midlands** (2,103 kha — the
single largest zone) at 202%, and **South Eastern** at a standout 361% (116 mm). The
exception is the **Great Southern corridor**: **Upper Great Southern** (502 kha) merely
above-average at decile 7.1 (132%), and **Lower Great Southern** (336 kha) the one
genuinely soft zone in the south — decile 4.3, 91% of median, a slight negative anomaly.

## South Australia — decile 10, 221% of median (75 mm)

The most uniformly wet state. Every wheat SD sits at decile 9–10. The major zones lead
it: **Eyre Peninsula** (852 kha) at 210%, **Yorke & Lower North** (363 kha) at 246%,
**Northern** (211 kha) at 249%, **Murray Lands** (278 kha) at 204%. No soft spots — a
comprehensively favourable establishment month.

## Victoria — decile 9, 166% of median (47 mm)

Wet and well-distributed, a notch below WA/SA. The smaller southern/central SDs are
saturated (Goulburn, Central Highlands, Western District all decile 10, 200%+). The two
big dryland zones are strong-but-not-extreme: the **Mallee** (524 kha) at decile 7.6
(132%) and the **Wimmera** (393 kha) at decile 8.6 (148%) — comfortably above average
everywhere that matters.

## New South Wales — decile 6.7, 125% of median (36 mm)

The transition state: above-average overall but mixed by region. The south is wet —
**Murray** (467 kha) at 173%, **Central West** (798 kha) at 145%, **Murrumbidgee**
(671 kha) at 120% — all decile 7–8. The north is the drag: **Northern** (838 kha) is
the largest below-median zone at decile 4.3 (74%), and **North Western** (1,084 kha —
the state's biggest) only modestly above median at decile 5.7 (121%). June rain has
improved the northern zones off a dry profile, but they remain the watch area (see SA2
evidence below — the dryness is largely a June lull on a wet May).

## Queensland — decile 4.8, 91% of median (14 mm)

The only state running below median, and the driest. **Darling Downs** (542 kha — the
dominant zone) is at decile 3.8 (81%, negative anomaly). **South West** (291 kha) is the
bright spot at decile 6.7 (132%). **Fitzroy** is very dry (decile 1.4, 23%) but small
(71 kha). Queensland's crop has had the weakest June and carries the thinnest moisture
profile of the five states.

**Bottom line.** South of the Murray it's an exceptional start — WA, SA and Victoria are
wet to very wet across virtually all wheat area, with WA's Lower Great Southern the lone
soft pocket. North of there the picture degrades: northern NSW and the Darling Downs have
had a fair-to-marginal June on limited stored moisture, leaving the northern zone reliant
on continued in-season rain to finish.

## SA2 evidence layer

Material wheat area only (≥40 kha). The three-window read is what separates a genuine
deficit from a cosmetic dry June.

### Genuine season-long deficits (dry on *all three* windows)

| SA2 | State · SD | Wheat kha | Jun | Mar→ | May→ |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Gnowangerup** | WA · Lower Gt Southern | 213 | 1.9 | 1.9 | 1.0 |
| **Wagin** | WA · Upper Gt Southern | 40 | 2.9 | 2.9 | 1.0 |
| Quirindi | NSW · Northern | 65 | 1.0 | 1.0 | 4.8 |

The only names dry on the season *and* on recent rain. **Gnowangerup (213 kha) is the
one that matters** — decile ~1–2 on every window, no profile and no establishment rain.
Wagin is the same WA Great Southern corridor at smaller scale. Quirindi is dry-season +
dry-June with only a mild May; small (65 kha).

topsoil → spring-finish exposure if it turns off.

## Net read

The only true SA2-level deficit in the whole belt is **WA's Gnowangerup–Wagin corridor**
(WA Great Southern). Everything that looked dry up north (Moree, Inglewood, Walgett,
Balonne) is a quiet June sitting on a *wet May*, and the Queensland names with low June
reads (Roma, Tara, Narrabri) have full season profiles. The dry-June signal in the north
is real but largely cosmetic — Gnowangerup is the exception that's genuinely short.
