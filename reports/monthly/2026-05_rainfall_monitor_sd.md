## Headline — a sharp east–west split

May delivered a **decisive break across the eastern grainbelt and a disappointing one in the west.** NSW, Queensland, Victoria and South Australia all finished the month wet to very wet (state deciles **D9–D10**, 148–278% of median), recharging profiles and setting up an excellent seeding window. **Western Australia was the lone dry state** (D3, 72% of median), with the dryness concentrated in its two largest grain regions — the Midlands and the Great Southern.

## Commentary

**The season has opened with a textbook east–west divergence.** For the eastern two-thirds of the national grainbelt, May was the kind of autumn break growers plan around — widespread, well above median, and timed for the heart of the winter-crop sowing window.

- **New South Wales (256% of median, D10).** A standout. Every grain SD finished wet, and the northern cropping zone was extreme: North Western at 343% (+63 mm), Central West 291% (+55 mm), South Eastern 252% (+63 mm). This is full-profile recharge and ideal establishment moisture across the state's 3.9 Mha. The only watch item is the *opposite* of drought — the wettest northern SDs (North Western, Central West) may see sowing delays or waterlogging on heavier country if rain persists into June.

- **Queensland (278%, D10).** The Darling Downs (303%, +47 mm) and South West (250%) banked excellent planting moisture for winter cereals and chickpea. Mackay's deficit is coastal/cane country and not material to the grain story.

- **Victoria (164%, D9).** The win is concentrated where it matters most for marginal cropping: the **Mallee at 251%** of its low ~19 mm median (+29 mm) and the Wimmera at 154% — a strong, reliable break for the northern dryland zone. Southern Victoria (Central Highlands, Western District, Barwon) sat near normal, which in already-reliable country is no concern.

- **South Australia (148%, D10).** Broad-based and including the typically marginal **Murray Lands at 229%** (+34 mm). Eyre Peninsula (135%), Yorke & Lower North (149%) and the South East (141%) all comfortably above median — a confident start across the SA grainbelt.

**Western Australia is the clear laggard and the one to watch.** At 72% of median (D3), WA's break has been patchy-to-poor across most of its grain country — and because WA carries the largest single-state wheat area (4.38 Mha), this matters for the national production and export picture.

- The deficit sits in the **biggest regions**: the **Midlands** (2.1 Mha, the single largest SD nationally) at 64% of median (D3, −8 mm), and the **Great Southern**, where **Upper Great Southern fell to 30% of median (D2, −25 mm)** and Lower Great Southern to 61% (D2). Only the eastern fringe (South Eastern, D7/117%) and the Central wheatbelt (essentially on its median, D6/101%) escaped.
- The practical read: much of the WA crop is going in dry or onto a marginal sub-soil moisture profile. Establishment risk is elevated in the Great Southern and northern/central wheatbelt, and the state now leans heavily on June follow-up rain. This is a genuine early-season downside risk worth tracking alongside the strongly favourable east.

**Bottom line for the grains desk:** the eastern crop is off to an excellent — locally even over-wet — start, while WA, the principal export state, has begun on the back foot. Watch the June rainfall for WA's Midlands and Great Southern; that, more than anything in the east, will shape how the early-season production narrative develops.

## Method & caveats

- **Geography.** True ABS 2011 Statistical Divisions, via the `sa2_2021_to_sd_2011_area_overlay` concordance. SD values are wheat-area-weighted means of member SA2s, so they describe **grain country within each SD**, not whole-of-division averages.
- **Rainfall.** SILO `daily_rain` 0.05° grid, summed over May 1–31 at each SA2 polygon centroid (nearest-grid-cell, not polygon-area-averaged). The 2026 grid was re-pulled on 2026-06-01 to capture the completed month.
- **Climatology.** Full-May totals 2005–2025 (21 years). Deciles are percentile-rank based (D10 = wettest, or near-wettest, of the 21 observed Mays); with only 21 years the decile scale is coarse, not a fitted distribution.
- **Scope.** 28 grain SDs above a 10,000 ha wheat floor (smallest retained: Barwon, 31 kha). Two SA SA2s (Le Hunte–Elliston, West Coast) dropped as off-grid nodata — neither carries wheat weight, so no impact.
- **Outputs.** `data/features/sd_2026_05_rainfall_review.csv`, `data/features/state_2026_05_rainfall_review.csv`, intermediate `data/features/sa2_2026_05_mtd.csv`; builder `scripts/build_sd_monthly_rainfall_review.py --year 2026 --month 5`.
