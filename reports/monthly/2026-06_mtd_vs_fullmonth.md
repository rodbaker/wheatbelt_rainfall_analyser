_Month-to-date rainfall through day 8 (1–8 June 2026), from the SILO daily grid, compared against the **unscaled** historical full-June median (2005–2025). The question this report answers: **how much of a normal whole June has already fallen in the first 8 days?**_

_Benchmark: 8/30 = 26.7% of the month has elapsed, so a region above ~27% is running ahead of its seasonal pace. "Pace" = (% of full June banked) ÷ 26.7%; >1× is ahead, <1× is behind. All state/SD figures are wheat-area weighted (kha). Two coastal SA2s with negative SILO sentinel sums (Le Hunte – Elliston, West Coast (SA)) are excluded._

## Takeaways

- **National pace 1.42× (38% of a full June banked in eight days)** — but this is an average over a strongly split country, not broad wetness.
- **Ahead of pace:** South Australia (64%) and Victoria (62%) broadly; southern NSW (Murray, Murrumbidgee, Central West).
- **Behind pace and area-heavy — the regions to watch:** WA **Midlands** (2,103 kha @ 19%), NSW **North Western + Northern** (~1.9 Mha @ 20% / 5%), and **all of Queensland's winter crop** (Darling Downs / South West @ ~0–1%). These are large areas that have banked almost none of a normal June.
- The wettest signal lands on SA's mid-north/Yorke and Vic's central districts; the largest *area* (WA Midlands, NSW north, Qld) is where the early-season deficit sits.

## Method & caveats

- **Coverage:** SILO daily grid, 1–8 June 2026 (month-to-date through day 8). SA2 centroid → nearest grid cell → daily sum, identical method to the canonical monthly extractor.
- **Baseline:** historical full-June totals, 2005–2025, per SA2; aggregated to SD/state using wheat crop-area weights. The "full-June median" columns are the **unscaled** climatology, so the "% of full June" figures are exact.
- **Pace is a ratio, not a forecast.** This is an eight-day snapshot — roughly a quarter of the month — so deciles/pace are volatile and behind-pace regions can close the gap quickly with a single front. No month-end outcome is implied.
- **Source files:** `data/features/sa2_2026_06_mtd.csv` (raw MTD), `data/features/{sd,state,sa2}_2026_06_rainfall_review.csv` (aggregations). Generated with `scripts/build_sd_monthly_rainfall_review.py --year 2026 --month 6`; full-June medians recovered by unscaling the review's scaled baseline by 30/8.
